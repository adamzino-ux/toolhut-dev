How to Use This Macro Calculator

Enter your age, sex, height, weight, and activity level. Select your goal — cutting (weight loss), maintaining, or bulking (muscle gain) — then choose a macro split or customize your own percentages. Hit Calculate My Macros to see your personalized daily calorie target and exact grams of protein, carbohydrates, and fat.

Why This Matters

Hitting a calorie target alone won't get you the results you want — the source of those calories matters enormously. Someone eating 2,400 calories worth of pizza will have a very different body composition outcome than someone hitting the same calories with optimized macros.

Consider: a 175 lb man trying to build muscle needs roughly 175–200g of protein per day — yet most people eat less than half that. Protein is uniquely satiating, protects lean mass during a cut, and triggers muscle protein synthesis. Getting it wrong is the #1 reason people lose muscle instead of fat when dieting.

Carbohydrates fuel high-intensity training — if you're doing weightlifting or HIIT 4+ days a week, cutting carbs too low will tank your performance. Conversely, someone sedentary doing light cardio can thrive on a low-carb approach. Fat is essential for hormonal health, vitamin absorption, and joint lubrication — never drop below ~20% of total calories.

This calculator uses the Mifflin-St Jeor equation — the most validated formula for estimating resting metabolic rate — combined with an activity multiplier to give you your Total Daily Energy Expenditure (TDEE).

How It's Calculated

Step 1: Basal Metabolic Rate (Mifflin-St Jeor)

  • Men: BMR = (10 × kg) + (6.25 × cm) − (5 × age) + 5
  • Women: BMR = (10 × kg) + (6.25 × cm) − (5 × age) − 161

Step 2: TDEE = BMR × Activity Multiplier (1.2 to 1.9)

Step 3: Goal Adjustment — Cut: TDEE − 500 kcal (~1 lb/week loss) | Bulk: TDEE + 300 kcal (~0.5 lb/week gain) | Maintain: TDEE

Step 4: Macro Grams — Each macro percentage × target calories ÷ calories per gram (protein: 4 kcal/g, carbs: 4 kcal/g, fat: 9 kcal/g)

Tips & Common Mistakes

  • Don't crash diet: A 500 kcal deficit is the sweet spot. Bigger deficits cause muscle loss and metabolic adaptation.
  • Track protein first: Hit your protein target before worrying about carbs and fat — protein is the most important lever for body composition.
  • Adjust every 2–4 weeks: As you lose or gain weight, your TDEE changes. Recalculate monthly.
  • Don't fear carbs on training days: Carb timing around workouts (pre/post) significantly impacts performance and recovery.
  • Activity multipliers are estimates: Most people overestimate their activity level — when in doubt, go one tier lower and adjust based on progress.

Frequently Asked Questions

What's the difference between TDEE and BMR?

Your BMR (Basal Metabolic Rate) is the calories your body burns at complete rest — just to keep organs functioning. TDEE (Total Daily Energy Expenditure) multiplies your BMR by your activity level to account for movement, exercise, and daily tasks. Your macro targets are based on TDEE, not BMR.

How much protein do I actually need to build muscle?

Research consistently shows 0.7–1.0g per pound of body weight (1.6–2.2g per kg) is optimal for muscle protein synthesis in people who resistance train. Beginners can build muscle at the lower end; advanced lifters in a caloric deficit benefit from the higher end. This calculator uses approximately 1g/lb as a strong default for bulking.

Should my macros change on rest days vs. training days?

Carb cycling — eating more carbs on training days and fewer on rest days — can be effective, but it adds complexity. For most people, consistent daily macros produce excellent results. Once you've mastered hitting daily targets, carb cycling is a useful next step. Keep protein consistent every day regardless.

Is the ketogenic macro split effective for weight loss?

Keto (very low carb, high fat) works for weight loss primarily because it often reduces appetite and total calorie intake. It is not metabolically superior to other diets when calories are matched. If you enjoy high-fat foods and find keto sustainable, it can be a great tool — but it's not necessary for fat loss. The "best" diet is the one you can stick to.
